Compound Interest

A core principal of finance is this: Provided money can earn interest, any amount of money is worth more the sooner it is received. This is also knows as the time value of money, and incredibly important to understand.

Compounding

Say you put $100 in a savings account that earned you 4% interest per year. By the end of the year, you would be left with $104 (+$4). Leave it there another year and you would earn 4% on top of the $104, giving you $108.16 (+$4.16). This idea of earning interest on top of interest is called compounding.

Basically, the longer you leave the $100, the higher your interest will get.

Everyday applications

Saving a $100/month into a savings account with interest: Instead of making a lump sum investment $100 at the end of each month, you can split it into two investments of $50 in week 2 and week 4. This is because the first investment in week 2 will earn interest up until week 4, and after you make your second investment, you will end up with slighly more than $100. Cool huh?!
