Budgeting and Saving

Budgeting and saving are going to be one of the two most important steps you're going to have to undertake to ensure you have a good financial future, so let's start!

What is a budget?

A budget is typically a spreadsheet that shows a detailed estimate of how much money you expect to earn and how much you expect to spend in a specified amount of time, usually a month.

How to make a budget?

There are a lot of resources online that you can use to create a budget, but you can start with simple pen and paper! All budgets should include these components:

5 components of a budget
After tax income Can be estimated with past paycheques, paystubs, etc...
Fixed expenses Rent, internet, cell phone, utility, subscription, insurance, ect...
Variable expenses Groceries, dining out, gas, clothing, gifts, entertainment, etc...
Unexpected expenses Vacation, buying a car, etc...
Savings What is left over after your expenses, can be directed to other stuff

Here is a simple example of a budget. Your own budget will most likely be a little more involved.

Perhaps the most important information you can glean from a budget is how much you're spending compared to how much you're earning. When you make more than you spend, you end up with a surplus at the end of the month, whih you can put towards your goals. If you spend more than you earn, you end up with a deficit, which is indicative of poor financial health and is unsustainable long term.

Why budget?

Creating a budget gives you a great idea of your financial health, and whether you are on a sustainable path in terms of your spending.
